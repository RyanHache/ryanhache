What you are building

This is a repeatable operating system with memory, connected tools, reusable agent skills, approval rules, and a weekly rhythm. The goal is not to become a prompt engineer. Your goal is to define the job once, review exceptions, and improve the system from real outcomes.

Step 1 — Operating context

Target

Customers with auto-repair balances 30–100 days overdue.

Offer/action

A clear balance reminder plus a secure remote payment path.

Bottleneck

Monthly paper statements and no text/email cadence.

Approval rule

Approved routine reminders may run automatically; disputes, hardship, unusual balances, and legal escalation require human review.

Why we are doing this: Top operators do not ask AI random questions. They give AI context, rules, examples, and a job.

Step 2 — Connected apps

  • Control database: a secure CRM table, Airtable, or Google Sheet containing approved operational fields.
  • Accounting source: a controlled QuickBooks overdue-invoice export from the local computer.
  • Messaging: a business SMS and email service with consent, opt-out, and delivery logging.
  • Payment: processor-hosted payment links or approved e-transfer instructions.
  • Approval queue: one view for disputes, failed delivery, wrong contacts, and nonstandard cases.
  • Knowledge base: approved tone, shop identity, payment policy, escalation rules, and examples.
Why we are doing this: A chatbot can suggest what to do. A connected agent can read, write, update, schedule, and prepare the next action.

Step 4 — Reusable agent skills

These are one-time setup instructions for reusable jobs, not daily prompts.

receivables-invoice-intake

Does: validates an accountant-approved QuickBooks export and creates clean invoice records. Inputs: CSV export, field mapping, exclusion rules. Tools: secure file intake and control database. Workflow: validate → deduplicate → flag missing contacts → write records. Outputs: import report and exception list. Guardrails: never modifies QuickBooks; rejects malformed or unexplained rows.

overdue-follow-up-orchestrator

Does: prepares the correct 30-, 60-, or 90-day reminder. Inputs: approved records, templates, cadence rules. Tools: database, SMS, email, approval queue. Workflow: select eligible records → verify status → create/send approved message → log result. Outputs: messages, delivery status, next actions. Guardrails: pause paid, disputed, opted-out, or low-confidence records.

payment-path-assistant

Does: inserts the correct secure pay-now route. Inputs: invoice ID, approved processor link pattern, e-transfer policy. Tools: payment provider and messaging templates. Workflow: select route → validate destination → insert link/instructions → log. Outputs: payment-ready reminder. Guardrails: never handles card or bank credentials and never invents a link.

weekly-receivables-review

Does: summarizes balances, payments, replies, promises, disputes, and stalled accounts. Inputs: control database and delivery logs. Tools: reporting view. Workflow: reconcile → calculate → surface exceptions → recommend next actions. Outputs: owner and accountant brief. Guardrails: labels estimates and links every claim to source records.

Why we are doing this: Reusable skills move you from “AI helped me once” to “AI runs part of my workflow every week.”

Step 5 — Weekly runbook

  1. Monday: accountant exports the approved overdue-invoice list; intake validates it.
  2. Daily: orchestrator sends only eligible approved reminders and logs delivery.
  3. Daily: owner or accountant reviews disputes, promises, bounced contacts, and unusual cases.
  4. Friday: review the receivables brief, reconcile payments in QuickBooks, and approve next actions.
  5. Monthly: compare dollars collected, response rate, payment-link usage, exceptions, and time saved; adjust cadence carefully.
Why we are doing this: Consistency is where leverage compounds. The same workflow every week creates cleaner data, better memory, and better outcomes.

Step 6 — Guardrails

  • Automate routine reminders only after templates and eligibility rules are approved.
  • Require human approval for disputes, hardship, legal escalation, payment terms, and unusual balances.
  • Keep customer financial data in approved systems; never paste payment credentials into AI.
  • Respect consent and opt-out requirements for texts and emails.
  • Reconcile payment status with QuickBooks before any follow-up.
  • Do not let the system make legal, credit, or collections determinations.
