What you are building

This is a repeatable operating system with memory, connected tools, reusable agent skills, approval rules, and a weekly rhythm. The goal is not to become a prompt engineer. Your goal is to define the job once, let safe routine work move faster, and keep the team focused on the guest experience.

Step 1 — Operating context

Target

Campground guests asking operational questions before or during their stay.

Offer/action

Fast, accurate text replies from one public business number plus a simple review ask after the stay.

Bottleneck

Hundreds of texts land on one personal phone and the approved answers mostly live in people's heads.

Approval rule

Routine approved FAQs may be answered automatically; bookings, complaints, exceptions, and unclear requests require human review.

Why we are doing this: Top operators do not ask AI random questions. They give AI context, rules, examples, and a job.

Step 2 — Connected apps

  • Shared business texting: one public number and team inbox for inbound guest messages.
  • Knowledge base: approved store hours, pump-out process, firewood rules, quiet hours, and common guest answers.
  • Reservation memory: a simple reservation or guest-status log so the team sees context before replying.
  • Review trigger: a post-stay or checkout workflow that launches the Google review request.
  • Approval queue: one place for complaints, unclear requests, and anything that needs a person.
  • Reporting layer: a weekly summary of message volume, escalations, and review results.
Why we are doing this: A chatbot can suggest what to do. A connected agent can read, write, update, schedule, and prepare the next action.

Step 4 — Reusable agent skills

These are one-time setup instructions for reusable jobs, not daily prompts.

guest-text-intake

Does: reads inbound texts from the business number, tags the question type, and sends safe routine requests into the approved answer flow. Inputs: incoming message, guest context, routing rules. Tools: texting inbox, database, approval queue. Workflow: ingest → classify → answer or escalate → log. Outputs: response action and next step. Guardrails: never invents policy exceptions or books anything without a human rule.

campground-faq-knowledge-sync

Does: turns approved operational knowledge into reusable answer blocks. Inputs: hours, firewood policy, pump-out process, quiet hours, staff-approved updates. Tools: knowledge base and review surface. Workflow: update → version → approve → publish. Outputs: live FAQ memory for both staff and AI-assisted replies. Guardrails: changes require owner approval and every answer must map back to a source note.

review-request-orchestrator

Does: sends the right Google review ask after the stay. Inputs: stay-complete trigger, approved review template, guest contact route. Tools: messaging system and reporting log. Workflow: select eligible guest → send review ask → log outcome → queue follow-up if needed. Outputs: review-request status and performance data. Guardrails: respects opt-out rules and never sends when the stay is unresolved.

weekly-guest-ops-review

Does: summarizes inbound text volume, common requests, unanswered questions, escalations, and review outcomes. Inputs: messaging log and review log. Tools: reporting surface. Workflow: reconcile → categorize → summarize → recommend improvements. Outputs: weekly owner brief. Guardrails: labels estimates and links every claim to the source queue.

Why we are doing this: Reusable skills move you from “AI helped me once” to “AI runs part of your workflow every week.”

Step 5 — Weekly runbook

  1. Before the week starts: update the approved FAQ answers for hours, firewood, pump-out, and seasonal changes.
  2. Daily: let routine questions auto-answer from the business number and review the escalation queue for bookings, complaints, and exceptions.
  3. Daily: note new repeat questions so the knowledge base gets better instead of staying in people's heads.
  4. After checkout or stay completion: trigger the Google review request for eligible guests.
  5. Weekly: review text volume, escalation themes, response coverage, and review performance; approve the next improvements.
Why we are doing this: Consistency is where leverage compounds. The same workflow every week creates cleaner data, better memory, and better outcomes.

Step 6 — Guardrails

  • Auto-answer only routine operational questions with approved knowledge.
  • Require human review for bookings, cancellations, complaints, refunds, and unclear requests.
  • Keep guest identities, reservation details, and payment-sensitive information in approved business systems.
  • Review seasonal policy changes before they go live in the knowledge base.
  • Do not let the system make hospitality-policy exceptions or promise unavailable services.
  • Pause review requests for unresolved guest issues.
