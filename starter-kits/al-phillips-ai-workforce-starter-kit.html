Personal Agentic AI Operating System

Hi Al Lead generation OS First 30 days

Let's build your AI Prospecting Operating System.

This is your step-by-step guide for building a repeatable agentic workflow that finds medical-center prospects, scores them, writes the best ones to your sheet or CRM, and prepares outreach for your approval.

Start the build View opportunity map
What you are building

A small AI workforce with memory, tools, repeatable skills, and a weekly operating rhythm.

The goal is not to become a prompt engineer. The goal is to install a system you can run every week with simple commands, review queues, and connected apps.

Step 1 - Give your AI the right operating context

Before your agents do any work, define what a good prospect looks like.

Your first AI leverage move is to stop starting from a blank chat. Give your agents a reusable operating context they can use every time they research, score, write, and follow up.

Why we are doing this: Top operators do not ask AI random questions. They give AI context, rules, examples, and a job. That is what turns AI from a chatbot into a worker.
Context 01

Your target buyer

Small U.S. medical centers and clinics, usually around ten employees, with HIPAA governance exposure.

Context 02

Your offer

Practical IT consulting around HIPAA compliance governance, risk reduction, and operational clarity.

Context 03

Your bottleneck

Finding the right prospects and converting warm replies into booked conversations.

Context 04

Your rule

Agents can research, score, draft, and organize. You approve outbound messages and sensitive claims.

Step 2 - Connect the apps your agents need

Your AI OS needs tools, not just instructions.

Once the context is defined, connect the places where work actually happens: prospect research, your control sheet, your CRM, your calendar, your approved message library, and your follow-up queue.

Why we are doing this: A chatbot can suggest what to do. A connected agent can read, write, update, schedule, and prepare the next action. This is the difference between advice and leverage.
Control database

Google Sheets or Airtable

This becomes the operating database your agents write to and read from.

Connect: Google Sheets MCP/API or Airtable API.
CRM memory

HighLevel, HubSpot, or your current CRM

Use this for qualified contacts, tasks, call outcomes, and follow-up stages.

Connect: CRM MCP/API or import workflow.
Research surface

Web search and browser research

Your agents need source URLs before they can score a prospect with confidence.

Connect: browser/search tool with source capture.
Outreach queue

LinkedIn approval workflow

Your agents prepare connection requests and DMs. You approve before sending.

Connect: manual review, CRM sequence, or compliant outreach tool.
Follow-up layer

Gmail and Google Calendar

Use these for resource emails, reminders, call prep, and scheduled next actions.

Connect: Gmail and Calendar MCP/API.
Knowledge base

Your approved offer context

Store your ICP, claims you are comfortable making, objections, resources, and winning messages.

Connect: local files, Drive, Notion, or a vector store.

Step 3 - Build the operating database

The sheet is where your AI workforce keeps score.

Start with a spreadsheet before custom software. It gives you visibility into what the agents are doing, makes mistakes easy to catch, and gives the system a clean place to store memory.

Why we are doing this: AI gets more useful when it has durable memory. If the work only lives in chat history, it is hard to audit, improve, or hand off to another tool.

Step 4 - Install reusable agent skills

Use these setup instructions once so your agents can run repeatable workflows.

These are not daily prompts. These are the instructions you or your AI builder can use to create reusable skills/scripts inside your Agentic AI Operating System.

Why we are doing this: Reusable skills are how you move from "AI helped me once" to "AI runs part of my business workflow every week." The skill keeps the role, rules, inputs, outputs, and tools consistent.

Skill 01

Medical Center Prospect Finder

Build a reusable skill named medical-center-prospect-finder for Al Phillips.

What this skill does:
Finds U.S. medical centers, clinics, and small healthcare practices that may be relevant for Al's HIPAA compliance governance offer.

Inputs Al can provide:
- geography
- specialty or clinic type
- max prospects per run
- source preferences
- exclusion criteria

Connected tools:
- web search or browser research
- Google Sheets or Airtable writer
- optional CRM lookup to avoid duplicates

Workflow:
1. Search for candidate organizations.
2. Open public source pages and capture evidence links.
3. Identify likely decision maker roles when available.
4. Exclude hospitals, large systems, and irrelevant organizations unless Al requests them.
5. Write new candidates into the Prospects sheet with status = New and source URLs.

Outputs:
- prospect rows in the sheet
- source URLs
- decision-maker role hypotheses
- confidence level
- duplicate checks

Guardrails:
- Use only public business information.
- Do not infer private compliance problems.
- Do not create outreach drafts in this skill.
- Never add a prospect without evidence links.

Skill 02

HIPAA Governance Lead Scorer

Build a reusable skill named hipaa-governance-lead-scorer for Al Phillips.

What this skill does:
Reads unscored prospect rows and scores them for fit with Al's ideal client profile.

Inputs Al can provide:
- sheet name
- status filter, usually New
- minimum evidence threshold
- scoring rubric version

Connected tools:
- Google Sheets or Airtable reader/writer
- browser/search for evidence verification when needed
- CRM lookup to check prior contact history

Scoring rubric:
1 point: medical center, clinic, specialty practice, or healthcare provider group.
1 point: appears close to the small-practice target where governance matters but internal resources are limited.
1 point: likely decision maker can be identified.
1 point: public signals suggest HIPAA governance surface area.
1 point: there is a credible outreach angle Al can use without exaggeration.

Workflow:
1. Read rows with status = New.
2. Score each row from 1-5.
3. Assign confidence: Low, Medium, or High.
4. Add reasoning and source notes.
5. Set status to Qualified Review when score is 4-5.
6. Set status to Research More when source evidence is weak.
7. Set status to Disqualified when score is below 3.

Guardrails:
- Do not claim the prospect has a HIPAA problem.
- Separate evidence from inference.
- Do not write outreach messages.

Skill 03

Outreach Brief Builder

Build a reusable skill named outreach-brief-builder for Al Phillips.

What this skill does:
Turns approved high-fit prospects into outreach briefs and message drafts for Al's review.

Inputs Al can provide:
- sheet name
- status filter, usually Qualified Review or Approved Prospect
- message type: connection request, first DM, resource bridge, follow-up, call ask

Connected tools:
- Google Sheets or Airtable reader/writer
- approved offer context and message library
- optional CRM contact context

Workflow:
1. Read prospects with fit score 4-5 and sufficient evidence.
2. Create a brief explaining why the prospect is relevant.
3. Draft one connection request and one first DM.
4. Draft one resource bridge for the "too busy" objection.
5. Write drafts into the sheet and set status to Needs Human Approval.
6. Do not send messages automatically.

Output fields:
- Outreach Angle
- Draft Message
- Human Review Notes
- Approval Status
- Suggested Next Action Date

Guardrails:
- No fearmongering.
- No legal advice.
- No fake personalization.
- No claims that Al knows their compliance status.
- Al approves or edits before anything is sent.

Skill 04

Follow-Up Orchestrator

Build a reusable skill named follow-up-orchestrator for Al Phillips.

What this skill does:
Keeps warm LinkedIn conversations from slipping by creating the next best action and follow-up schedule.

Inputs Al can provide:
- sheet or CRM view
- reply text or reply category
- last touch date
- current status
- resource sent, if any

Connected tools:
- Google Sheets or Airtable reader/writer
- CRM task creation
- Gmail draft creation when email is the right next step
- Google Calendar task/reminder when needed

Workflow:
1. Read active conversations due for follow-up.
2. Classify reply intent: interested but busy, not now, needs education, asked a question, unclear, disqualified.
3. Create the next best action.
4. Draft the next message or resource note.
5. Create or update CRM task.
6. Update Next Action Date and status.

Guardrails:
- Never pressure the prospect.
- Preserve relationship quality.
- Keep Al's review in the loop for outbound messaging.
- Do not send messages automatically unless Al explicitly approves that channel and rule.

Skill 05

Weekly Pipeline Review

Build a reusable skill named weekly-pipeline-review for Al Phillips.

What this skill does:
Gives Al a weekly operating review of his AI-assisted lead-generation system.

Inputs Al can provide:
- prospect sheet
- CRM tasks and outcomes
- sent messages and replies, if available
- booked calls

Connected tools:
- Google Sheets or Airtable reader
- CRM reader
- Gmail/Calendar reader if used for follow-up and calls

Workflow:
1. Count new prospects found.
2. Count prospects scored 4-5.
3. Count approved outreach drafts.
4. Count connection requests, replies, follow-ups, and booked calls.
5. Identify where the system is leaking: research, scoring, approval, outreach, reply conversion, or follow-up.
6. Recommend one improvement for the next week.

Output:
- weekly summary
- bottleneck diagnosis
- next action list
- message or scoring improvements
- 3 examples of what worked

Step 5 - Run the weekly operating rhythm

Operate your AI OS with repeatable workflows, not random prompting.

Once the skills are installed, your weekly job is to run the right workflow, review the output, approve what should happen next, and let the system carry the state forward.

Why we are doing this: Consistency is where the leverage compounds. The same workflow every week creates cleaner data, better agent memory, better message examples, and better follow-up discipline.
Monday

Find and score

/run prospect-finder geography="United States" niche="medical centers" limit=50
/run lead-scorer status="New" min_confidence="Medium"
Tuesday

Build approval queue

/run outreach-brief-builder status="Qualified Review" min_score=4
/review outreach_queue
Wednesday

Move approved work

/sync approved_prospects to_crm=true
/prepare approved_linkedin_actions
Friday

Follow up and improve

/run follow-up-orchestrator due_before=today
/run weekly-pipeline-review

Your definition of done

Step 6 - Keep control of the system

Automate research, scoring, drafts, syncing, and reminders. Keep outbound sending accountable.

Your first build should prepare LinkedIn actions and follow-up work, then let you approve the final send. That protects relationship quality, avoids sloppy automation, and keeps the system aligned with whichever outreach tools and platform rules you choose to use.

Do not paste patient information, client private documents, breach details, protected health information, or confidential internal processes into a general AI tool. Start with public prospecting data, approved offer context, and reviewed messaging.
