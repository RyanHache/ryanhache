Receivables Follow-Up AI Workforce Opportunity Map

Turn a paper-based, once-a-month collection process into a consistent text-and-email follow-up system that helps customers pay without returning to the shop.

Primary objective

Collect 50 overdue invoices more consistently.

Current workflow

Paper records, monthly mailed statements, local QuickBooks.

Target customer

Auto-repair customers with balances 30–100 days overdue.

Business problem

Roughly $10,000–$20,000 may be tied up while the team stays focused on repairs.

Executive diagnosis

The problem is not a lack of customer contact information. The shop already has phone numbers, emails, and mailing addresses. The leak is cadence and convenience: follow-up happens about once a month by letter, and customers have no easy remote payment path.

LEAK 01

Slow cadence

Monthly letters allow forgotten balances to age.

LEAK 02

Payment friction

Customers generally pay only when they return in person.

LEAK 03

Manual burden

The accountant spends about five hours monthly preparing mail.

Top 3 AI workforce opportunities

01 — FIRST DEPLOYMENT

Receivables Follow-Up Orchestrator

Prepare and send approved text and email reminders at 30, 60, and 90 days, pause when paid, and escalate exceptions.

  • Reads an approved overdue-invoice export
  • Creates polite cadence-based reminders
  • Logs delivery, replies, and next action

Impact: High · Feasibility: High · Speed: Fast · Oversight: Medium

02

Payment Path Assistant

Add a secure pay-now option or approved e-transfer instructions to each reminder.

  • Uses processor-hosted links
  • Never collects card data in chat
  • Routes disputes to a human

Impact: High · Feasibility: Medium · Speed: Medium · Oversight: High

03

Receivables Tracking Agent

Maintain a live list of balances, contact attempts, promises, disputes, and next steps.

  • Creates one operational queue
  • Surfaces aging and exceptions
  • Prepares a weekly owner summary

Impact: Medium · Feasibility: High · Speed: Fast · Oversight: Low

Recommended AI workforce design

Invoice Intake Agent

Reads the accountant-approved QuickBooks export and writes normalized invoice records.

Follow-Up Orchestrator

Reads aging, contact details, and cadence rules; writes drafts or sends pre-approved templates; hands replies to the queue.

Payment Path Assistant

Inserts the correct secure payment link or e-transfer instructions without handling payment credentials.

Exception Review Agent

Flags disputes, hardship, wrong contact details, and promises to pay for human review.

Weekly Receivables Reporter

Summarizes dollars outstanding, movement, responses, payments, and overdue actions.

30-day implementation sequence

Week 1

Define the invoice schema, aging rules, approved language, payment instructions, and escalation boundaries.

Week 2

Install the intake and follow-up workflow using a safe QuickBooks export.

Week 3

Run a small approval queue, then send the first controlled batch.

Week 4

Review responses and payments, improve messaging, and connect the reporting layer.

Privacy and oversight

Keep customer financial details in approved business systems. Do not paste card numbers, banking credentials, or sensitive identity data into general AI tools. A human should review disputes, hardship cases, legal escalation, unusual balances, and any change to payment terms. The system may prepare and route work; it should not make legal or credit decisions.
